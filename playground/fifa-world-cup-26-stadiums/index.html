Toronto Stadium – BMO Field

Location: Toronto, Ontario, Canada

Capacity: ~45,000

Opened: 2007

Local team(s): Toronto FC (MLS)

FIFA World Cup 26™ fixtures: 5 group-stage matches + 1 Round of 32 tie

Toronto Stadium (BMO Field) is Canada's premier football-specific venue, originally constructed for the FIFA U-20 World Cup 2007™ and expanded significantly for the 2026 tournament. Home to Toronto FC, the stadium's steep stands and intimate design create one of the most atmospheric matchday settings in North America. Hosting six fixtures—including Canada's opening game—it stands at the heart of the nation's football journey and showcases Toronto's global, vibrant energy.

Mexico City Stadium – Estadio Azteca

Location: Mexico City, Mexico

Capacity: ~87,523

Opened: 1966

Local team(s): Club América (Liga MX)

FIFA World Cup 26™ fixtures: Opening match + multiple group-stage matches + knockout rounds

Mexico City Stadium (Estadio Azteca) is one of the most iconic venues in football history—host of the 1970 and 1986 FIFA World Cup finals, and soon to be the first to host a third. Home to Club América and revered by fans across the world, it has witnessed legends like Pelé and Maradona define eras. For 2026, it returns as a cathedral of world football, delivering unmatched atmosphere and historic significance.
